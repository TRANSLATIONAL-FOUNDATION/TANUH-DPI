TANUH: The AI Centre of Excellence in Healthcare

TANUH (Translational Artificial intelligence for Networked Universal Healthcare) is a Section 8 not-for-profit organization promoted by Indian Institute of Science (IISc). Funded by the Ministry of Education as the AI Centre of Excellence in Healthcare, TANUH is dedicated to advancing the Government of India vision of "Make AI in India and Make AI Work for India".

TANUH develops secure, scalable, and AI-enabled Digital Public Infrastructure (DPI) for healthcare, designed to support healthcare providers, insurers, and patients. The current suite of tools addresses the processing of clinical documents and insurance policies by converting unstructured information into structured data. For document adjudication, the platform also includes automated interactive forgery detection capabilities. In addition, a privacy filter framework is part of this DPI for automated anonymization and redaction of clinical documents and images to facilitate the clinical research and/or AI development.

All services within this DPI operate in a non-persistent manner: data is processed in real time and purged automatically, with no data retention. The platform is also designed for local, on-premises deployment, in accordance with stringent data privacy and protection requirements.

Through this infrastructure, TANUH aims to deliver an integrated ecosystem grounded in efficiency, security, and trust. TANUH AI technologies are purpose-built to enable intelligent and transformative healthcare solutions for India.
